Metformin - Food and Drug Interactions

Metformin (Glucophage) - Common diabetes medication

Food Interactions:

Safe to eat:
- Most fruits and vegetables
- Whole grains
- Lean proteins
- Low-fat dairy

Caution with alcohol:
- Limit alcohol consumption
- Alcohol + Metformin increases lactic acidosis risk
- Maximum: 1 drink/day for women, 2 drinks/day for men
- Never drink on empty stomach

Vitamin B12:
- Long-term Metformin use can reduce B12 absorption
- Consider B12 supplementation after 3-4 years
- Monitor B12 levels annually

Timing:
- Take with meals to reduce GI side effects
- If twice daily: breakfast and dinner
- Extended-release: once daily with evening meal

Drug Interactions:

Increases hypoglycemia risk when combined with:
- Insulin
- Sulfonylureas
- Other diabetes medications

May need dose adjustment with:
- Diuretics
- Corticosteroids
- Beta-blockers
- Calcium channel blockers

Contraindications:
- Severe kidney disease (eGFR <30)
- Liver disease
- Heart failure
- Recent heart attack or stroke

Side effects (usually temporary):
- Nausea, diarrhea (first 2 weeks)
- Metallic taste
- Reduced appetite

When to call doctor:
- Severe stomach pain
- Difficulty breathing
- Unusual muscle pain
- Extreme fatigue
